Building a Flight Simulator Control Box | Post Date: 10/08/2024

How will it work?

The core of this project is a very neat software called Mobiflight which allows you to easily interface an arduino style microcontroller with games such as Microsoft Flight Simulator 2020.
I've played with this software in the past and 3d printed a little button box to try it. Overall it was a very good experience, but my box was designed very quickly and haphazardly. The goal of this project is to revise my previous design in a more luxery manner.

What will it look like?

I plan to make the case out of wood, or plastic with a wood venier. I am designing the box to fit on my desk and be approximately one foot by three inches in size, and be as deep as needed. The front panel I plan to be aluminum with backlit labels, and for connectivity I plan to terminate a USB cable with a more robust DIN plug.
The scope of this control panel will be simple auxilery functions in the Cessna 152, mainly power control and lighting.

How will it be made?

I am planning to spend alot of time working out the details in my designed, before finalizing an assembly file for manufacture. I know I will be having the faceplates CNC machined through an online service, though I dont have one in mind yet. I usually use JLC PCB for circuitboard simply because its cheap and I've had good luck with them. The wooden case will proove the most difficult, though I may be able to use a local makerspace's CNC.
